# Example: Startup Decision — SaaS vs. Agency

**Scenario**: A solo founder with $40K savings, 6 months of runway, and 3 years of design consulting experience must decide whether to build a SaaS product or start a design agency.

---

## DECISION: Business Model Choice

**Date**: 2024-Q1  
**Type**: Strategic  
**Urgency**: High (runway limited)  
**Confidence**: Medium

---

## Goal

Choose a business model that generates $8K/month revenue within 6 months, given $40K savings and solo capacity.

---

## Constraints

**Hard**
- Budget: ≤ $40,000 total
- Timeline: Revenue within 6 months (before runway runs out)
- Team: Solo founder — no hiring in phase 1

**Soft**
- Preference for scalable business long-term
- Risk tolerance: Medium (has savings buffer, no dependents)

---

## Assumptions

| Assumption | Confidence | Risk if Wrong |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|
| Can close 3 agency clients in month 1–2 | Medium | Revenue delayed 2–3 months |
| SaaS can reach 100 paying users in 6 months | Low | Runway expires before profitability |
| Design market demand is strong | High | Low — well-validated |

---

## Options

### Plan A — Design Agency
Launch freelance/agency offering immediately. Land clients via network, LinkedIn, cold outreach. Target 3 retainer clients at $3K/month each = $9K/month by month 2.

**Strengths**: Fast revenue, proven skills, no product build required  
**Weaknesses**: Time-for-money trap, doesn't scale, founder becomes the product

### Plan B — SaaS Product (full build)
Build a B2B SaaS tool for designers. 3-month build, 3-month launch/grow. Target $8K MRR by month 6.

**Strengths**: Scalable, recurring revenue, high long-term upside  
**Weaknesses**: 0 revenue for 3 months, high execution risk, uncertain PMF

### Plan C — Agency-Funded SaaS (Hybrid)
Run agency for first 3 months (2 clients at $4K/month), use revenue + learnings to fund and inform a narrow SaaS product. Launch SaaS in month 4.

**Strengths**: Revenue from day 1, reduces burn, real customer insight informs product  
**Weaknesses**: Divided focus, slower SaaS launch, risk of agency consuming all time

---

## Evaluation

| Option | Feasibility (×0.25) | Impact (×0.30) | Risk (×0.20) | Cost (×0.15) | Time (×0.10) | **TOTAL** |
|--------|--------------------|--------------  |-------------|-------------|-------------|-----------|
| A — Agency | 5 → 1.25 | 3 → 0.90 | 4 → 0.80 | 5 → 0.75 | 5 → 0.50 | **4.20** |
| B — SaaS | 2 → 0.50 | 5 → 1.50 | 2 → 0.40 | 3 → 0.45 | 1 → 0.10 | **2.95** |
| C — Hybrid | 4 → 1.00 | 4 → 1.20 | 3 → 0.60 | 4 → 0.60 | 4 → 0.40 | **3.80** |

---

## Selected Plan

**Winner**: Plan A — Design Agency (Score: 4.20)

**Why**: With 6-month runway and solo capacity, revenue certainty is the priority. The agency model is executable immediately using proven skills. It generates the target $8K/month before runway expires, with minimal risk.

**Trade-off vs. Plan C (Hybrid)**: Plan A forgoes the long-term SaaS upside. Plan C is more strategic but splits focus and risks delivering neither revenue nor product.

**Why acceptable**: SaaS remains an option after runway is extended via agency revenue. Agency learning informs future product — not wasted time.

---

## Execution Steps

| # | Action | Owner | By When | Output |
|---|--------|-------|---------|--------|
| 1 | Define service offering + pricing | Founder | Week 1 | Service page + deck |
| 2 | Reach out to 50 warm contacts | Founder | Week 1–2 | 10 discovery calls |
| 3 | Close 2 retainer clients | Founder | Week 3–4 | $6K/month revenue |
| 4 | Systematize delivery (templates, SOPs) | Founder | Month 2 | Reusable delivery system |
| 5 | Evaluate SaaS opportunity from client patterns | Founder | Month 3 | Product hypothesis doc |

**First checkpoint**: Month 1 — 2 clients closed or pivot outreach strategy  
**First value**: Week 3–4 — first invoice sent

---

## Top Risks

| # | Risk | Score | Mitigation | Warning Signal |
|---|------|-------|-----------|---------------|
| 1 | Network doesn't convert to clients | 6 | Expand to cold outreach + LinkedIn ads (budget $500) | < 3 discovery calls by week 2 |
| 2 | Agency time prevents SaaS exploration | 4 | Block 4 hours/week for product research | No product work by month 3 |
| 3 | Founder burnout from client work | 4 | Cap at 2 clients in phase 1 | Working > 55 hrs/week consistently |

---

## Fallback Plan

**Trigger**: No clients closed by end of month 2  
**Fallback**: Switch to Plan C (Hybrid) — take 1 part-time contract client, spend remaining time building a narrow SaaS tool targeting the exact problem experienced in outreach  
**Owner**: Founder decides by day 60
