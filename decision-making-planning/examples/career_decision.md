# Example: Career Decision

**Scenario**: A UX designer with 5 years of experience has received 3 offers simultaneously: (A) Senior Designer at a fast-growing startup ($95K), (B) Lead Designer at an established agency ($110K), (C) Stay at current company with a promotion ($85K + equity). She values growth, work-life balance, and long-term financial upside.

---

## DECISION: Career Move Choice

**Date**: 2024-Q2  
**Type**: Personal / Career  
**Urgency**: High (offers expire in 2 weeks)  
**Confidence**: Medium

---

## Goal

Choose the role that maximizes career growth + compensation + wellbeing over the next 3–5 years.

---

## Constraints

**Hard**
- Must decide within 2 weeks
- Minimum acceptable salary: $85K (matched by all options)
- Cannot take a role requiring > 50 hrs/week consistently (caregiving responsibilities)

**Soft**
- Prefers a role with a path to Principal or Head of Design within 3 years
- Values working on product she believes in
- Prefers remote or hybrid

---

## Assumptions

| Assumption | Confidence | Risk if Wrong |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|
| Startup equity could be worth $200K+ in 3–5 years | Low | Equity worthless — startup fails |
| Agency work will build broad skills faster | Medium | Agency becomes execution-only, skills stagnate |
| Current company promotion is real and resourced | Medium | Promotion is underfunded or never materializes |

---

## Options

### Plan A — Senior Designer, Startup ($95K + 0.3% equity)
Fast-growing Series B, 60-person team. Direct report to CPO. Product: climate tech SaaS.

**Strengths**: High ownership, direct product impact, equity upside, mission-driven  
**Weaknesses**: Startup risk, no dedicated design team yet, likely > 45 hrs/week

### Plan B — Lead Designer, Agency ($110K, no equity)
Established 200-person agency. Manage 3 junior designers. Clients: Fortune 500 brands.

**Strengths**: Highest base salary, management experience, diverse client exposure  
**Weaknesses**: Client work is often execution-only, no product ownership, billable hour pressure

### Plan C — Stay + Promotion to Senior Designer ($85K + existing equity)
Current company promotes her to Senior, gives design system ownership.

**Strengths**: Known environment, no onboarding cost, relationship capital built, equity already vesting  
**Weaknesses**: Lowest salary, may plateau, comfort zone risk, unclear promotion timeline

---

## Evaluation

*Weights adjusted for personal goals: Growth 35%, Compensation 25%, Wellbeing 20%, Future Upside 20%*

| Option | Growth (×0.35) | Compensation (×0.25) | Wellbeing (×0.20) | Future Upside (×0.20) | **TOTAL** |
|--------|---------------|---------------------|------------------|-----------------------|-----------|
| A — Startup | 5 → 1.75 | 3 → 0.75 | 3 → 0.60 | 5 → 1.00 | **4.10** |
| B — Agency | 3 → 1.05 | 5 → 1.25 | 3 → 0.60 | 2 → 0.40 | **3.30** |
| C — Stay | 2 → 0.70 | 2 → 0.50 | 5 → 1.00 | 3 → 0.60 | **2.80** |

---

## Selected Plan

**Winner**: Plan A — Startup (Score: 4.10)

**Why**: Growth is the stated top priority, and the startup offers the highest growth ceiling: direct CPO access, ownership of product design from 0→1, and equity in a mission-driven company she believes in. The $15K salary gap vs. Plan B is offset by potential equity and faster career trajectory.

**Trade-off vs. Plan B (Agency)**: Gives up $15K base salary and management experience. The agency's management path is real — but it's managing designers on client briefs, not building product. That's a different kind of growth.

**Why acceptable**: On a 3–5 year horizon, the startup either succeeds (high financial + career upside) or fails (she has a strong "built design at Series B startup" story for her next role). Both outcomes serve the goal.

---

## Execution Steps

| # | Action | By When | Output |
|---|--------|---------|--------|
| 1 | Negotiate startup offer: request $100K base (closer to agency) | Day 1–3 | Counter-offer sent |
| 2 | Due diligence: 1 hour with startup CPO + 1 current team member | Day 3–5 | Culture + scope confirmed |
| 3 | Check equity terms: vesting schedule, cliff, dilution protection | Day 3–5 | Equity value assessed |
| 4 | Accept or decline based on negotiation outcome | Day 7 | Decision finalized |
| 5 | Graceful exit from current company (4-week notice) | Week 2–6 | Relationships preserved |

**Decision point**: If startup doesn't move on salary and equity terms are poor → reconsider Plan B

---

## Top Risks

| # | Risk | Score | Mitigation | Warning Signal |
|---|------|-------|-----------|---------------|
| 1 | Startup fails within 18 months | 6 | Confirm 18+ months runway in due diligence; build savings buffer | Layoffs, missed Series C |
| 2 | Role scope is narrower than presented | 4 | Get scope in writing; ask about current design process in interviews | Offer letter mentions "supporting" not "leading" |
| 3 | Hours exceed 50/week regularly | 4 | Explicitly discuss boundaries in final conversation with CPO | Hiring manager can't name a boundary |

---

## Fallback Plan

**Trigger**: Startup cancels offer, or due diligence reveals red flags (runway < 12 months, cultural mismatch)  
**Fallback**: Accept Plan B (Agency). Accept the management track and use client diversity to build breadth; revisit a product role in 18–24 months.  
**Owner**: Decision by Day 10 deadline
